Why XGBoost was selected

Data-driven reasoning behind the model choice

Highest ROC-AUC

AUC measures how well the model separates disease from no-disease at every decision threshold — more meaningful than raw accuracy for medical classifiers.

Best recall on disease class

Missing a real disease case costs more than a false alarm. XGBoost had the highest recall — fewest missed disease cases across all evaluation folds.

Captures feature interactions

Heart disease risk depends on combinations — chest pain type plus ST depression together. Gradient boosting captures these non-linear interactions better than logistic regression.

Lowest cross-validation variance

XGBoost had the lowest std deviation across 5 folds — meaning it generalises more reliably to unseen patients, not just the training split.

Feature importance — what drives predictions (SHAP)

Mean absolute SHAP value across all test patients. Chest pain type and max heart rate are the strongest predictors.
